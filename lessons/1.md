The letter for <strong>s</strong>ee is <mark>ğ‘•</mark>. Yep, it looks like a Latin S.

The letter for <strong>i</strong>n is <mark>ğ‘¦</mark>. It looks like the Latin i without the dot.


The letter for <strong>e</strong>ve is <mark>ğ‘°</mark>. This sound is similar to "in", except it is longer. This letter looks like someone tried to lengthen the ğ‘¦ and it broke in the middle!

The letter for <strong>z</strong>oo is <mark>ğ‘Ÿ</mark>. Yes, it's a reversed ğ‘•! Similar to the Latin s z. ğ‘• ğ‘Ÿ.


Compare these letters together: ğ‘• ğ‘Ÿ. Pronounce each of them: so, zoo. Notice that the <strong>z</strong>oo letter is lower on the line.


When you say "zzz", notice that your vocal chords vibrate. This is called a "voiced" sound. When you say "sss", your vocal chords don't vibrate. That is called an "unvoiced" sound.

In Shavian, most sounds that are unvoiced are <em>tall</em> and voiced sounds are <em>deep</dem>. Just remember that your vocal chords are low, so the letters are low!
